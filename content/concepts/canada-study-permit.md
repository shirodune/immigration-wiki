# Canada study permit

> Research summary only; not legal advice. Verify consequential decisions with IRCC, the applicable province or territory, and a qualified adviser where appropriate.

## Scope and currency

- **Jurisdiction:** Canada; Quebec and other province-specific requirements are not fully covered
- **Applicable dates:** Source pages do not state a single effective date; IRCC metadata was modified between 2026-01-26 and 2026-04-24
- **Last source check:** 2026-07-15
- **Audience:** Prospective and current international students

## Summary

A study permit is an IRCC-issued document that generally allows a foreign national to study at a Canadian designated learning institution. Eligibility, financial evidence, ongoing study obligations, and any authorization to work must each be satisfied; admission to a school does not by itself establish every immigration or post-graduation benefit. ^[raw/articles/canada-ircc-study-permit.md] ^[raw/articles/canada-ircc-study-permit-eligibility.md]

## Evidence

### Purpose and application

IRCC states that most foreign nationals need a study permit to study at a designated learning institution and advises applying before travel. Its landing page displayed a CAN$150 fee on 2026-07-15, while processing time varied by country. These operational figures can change. ^[raw/articles/canada-ircc-study-permit.md]

### General eligibility and continuing duties

The eligibility page lists DLI enrolment, sufficient funds, criminality and health requirements where applicable, and proof that the applicant will leave when the permit expires. During study, the student must progress toward program completion, respect permit conditions, and stop studying if the requirements are no longer met. ^[raw/articles/canada-ircc-study-permit-eligibility.md]

### Financial support

Applicants must show funds, without relying on work in Canada, for tuition, living expenses for themselves and accompanying family, and transportation. IRCC requires resources for the first year and, for longer programs, a plan for the remaining duration. Evidence examples include paid tuition or housing, bank funds, guaranteed investment certificates, loans, funding letters, and scholarships; the list is not exhaustive. ^[raw/articles/canada-ircc-study-permit-financial-support.md]

The source says living-expense amounts are updated annually. This page therefore does not preserve a numeric threshold; the current IRCC amount and any Quebec or local visa-office requirements must be checked for the application date. ^[raw/articles/canada-ircc-study-permit-financial-support.md]

### Conditional off-campus work

Off-campus work is not automatic. The reviewed IRCC page lists cumulative requirements including eligible full-time DLI study, a qualifying program of at least six months, valid study-permit status, printed work authorization, and a Social Insurance Number. Eligible students may work up to 24 hours per week during regular terms and unlimited hours during scheduled breaks while they remain eligible. ^[raw/articles/canada-ircc-work-off-campus.md]

## Interpretation

- Admission and DLI status are necessary in common cases but do not guarantee a permit or post-graduation work-permit eligibility.
- Work authorization cannot be treated as a substitute for the financial-capacity evidence required before study.
- The student’s permit wording and current IRCC guidance matter more than a general description of work rights.

## Limitations and open questions

- The source set is federal operational guidance, not a complete reproduction of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act or Regulations.
- Study-without-permit exceptions, provincial attestation rules, Quebec requirements, dependants, medical examinations, and post-graduation pathways need dedicated sources.
- Fee, processing, financial, and work-hour information is time-sensitive and should be rechecked immediately before use.
