# New Zealand — Immigration New Zealand Working on a student visa

## Citation

Immigration New Zealand. “Working on a student visa.” Accessed 2026-07-15. [Original page](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/study/once-you-have-a-student-visa/working-on-a-student-visa/).

## Authority and scope

Immigration New Zealand is the government visa authority. This page describes conditional work rights attached to New Zealand student visas.

## Original summary

The page says work permission depends on course and individual visa conditions. A student may be allowed to work part-time for up to 25 hours per week and full-time during specified study breaks and the Christmas–New Year period. It identifies potentially eligible groups, including certain tertiary, postgraduate, English-language, secondary-school, exchange, and practical-experience students. It states that work conditions can only be granted from age 16 and that students must check their eVisa or visa letter.

## Consequential claims supported

- **Claim:** A student visa may permit up to 25 hours of part-time work per week and full-time work during specified breaks.
  - **Jurisdiction:** New Zealand
  - **Applicable date:** Not stated on page
  - **Accessed:** 2026-07-15
  - **Limitations:** Permission is conditional; the individual eVisa or visa letter controls and not every student is eligible.
- **Claim:** Student-visa work conditions can only be granted to students aged 16 or older.
  - **Jurisdiction:** New Zealand
  - **Applicable date:** Not stated on page
  - **Accessed:** 2026-07-15
  - **Limitations:** Additional course, level, age, school, and parental-permission rules can apply.

## Limitations

The page contains category-specific rules for tertiary, research, English-language, secondary, exchange, and practical-experience study. It does not replace the individual visa conditions. A variation of conditions or different visa may be required when circumstances change. No clear modification or effective date was exposed.
