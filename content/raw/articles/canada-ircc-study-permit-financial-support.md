# Canada — IRCC Study permit proof of financial support

## Citation

Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada. “Study permit: Get the right documents — Proof of financial support.” Issued 2024-04-22 and modified 2026-01-26 in page metadata. Accessed 2026-07-15. [Original page](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/study-canada/study-permit/get-documents/financial-support.html).

## Authority and scope

IRCC is the Canadian federal immigration authority. This page explains the costs a study-permit applicant must cover and gives examples of acceptable financial evidence.

## Original summary

Applicants must show enough money without relying on work in Canada to cover tuition, living expenses for themselves and accompanying family members, and transportation to and from Canada. The page says applicants must demonstrate resources for the first year and, for longer programs, explain how they plan to pay for the full duration. It lists examples of evidence, including paid tuition or housing, bank funds, guaranteed investment certificates, loans, bank statements, funding letters, scholarships, and Canadian-funded programs, while emphasizing that the examples are neither exhaustive nor all mandatory.

## Consequential claims supported

- **Claim:** Financial proof must address tuition, living expenses, and transportation without relying on Canadian employment.

## Limitations

The page states that living-expense amounts are updated annually. This record intentionally does not freeze those figures; they must be checked on the official page for the relevant application date. Country or regional visa-office instructions may require additional documents.
