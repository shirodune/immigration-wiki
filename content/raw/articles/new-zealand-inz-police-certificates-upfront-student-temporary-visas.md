# New Zealand — INZ Police certificates required upfront for student and temporary visas

## Citation

Immigration New Zealand. “Police certificates required upfront for student and temporary visas.” Published 2026-07-01. Accessed 2026-07-17. [Original announcement](https://www.immigration.govt.nz/about-us/news-centre/police-certificates-required-upfront-for-student-and-temporary-visas/).

## Authority and scope

Immigration New Zealand is the government visa authority. This announcement explains how INZ handles required police certificates for student and other temporary-visa applications.

## Original summary

INZ states that when a police certificate is required, the applicant must upload it with the application. A receipt or confirmation of having requested one is generally not accepted, and INZ will not usually contact the applicant for a missing certificate. An application may be declined or, in some circumstances, granted for a shorter period. The announcement describes different possible consequences for offshore and onshore applicants and an exception for Fiji, Hong Kong, and Israel, where issuing authorities send certificates directly to INZ and proof of application may be uploaded.

## Consequential claims supported

- **Claim:** A required police certificate must generally be provided when a student or other temporary-visa application is lodged.
  - **Jurisdiction:** New Zealand
  - **Applicable date:** Announcement published 2026-07-01; separate commencement date not stated
  - **Accessed:** 2026-07-17
  - **Limitations:** A police certificate is not required in every case; current category instructions and the applicant’s circumstances determine the requirement.
- **Claim:** A missing required certificate may lead to refusal or a shorter visa, and INZ will not usually request it after lodgement.
  - **Jurisdiction:** New Zealand
  - **Applicable date:** Announcement published 2026-07-01
  - **Accessed:** 2026-07-17
  - **Limitations:** Onshore and offshore handling differs, and the announcement describes exceptions and discretionary outcomes rather than a universal result.

## Limitations

This is an operational announcement, not the complete immigration instructions. It does not establish which applicants need certificates, certificate validity periods, issuing-country procedures, or every exception. Applicants from Fiji, Hong Kong, and Israel have a distinct submission process described on the page. The announcement did not state a separate effective date, so the live category instructions should be checked before lodgement.
