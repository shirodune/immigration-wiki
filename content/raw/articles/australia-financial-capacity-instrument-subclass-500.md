# Australia — Financial capacity instrument for subclass 500

## Citation

Australia. _Migration (LIN 19/198: Evidence of financial capacity—Subclass 500 Visa and Subclass 590 Visa) Instrument 2019_, compilation F2024C00445 C02. Compilation date 2024-05-10. Federal Register of Legislation. Accessed 2026-07-15. [Official text](https://www.legislation.gov.au/F2019L01366/latest/text).

## Authority and scope

This legislative instrument is made under Schedule 2 clauses 500.214, 500.313, and 590.216 of the _Migration Regulations 1994_. The Register identified it as in force and current on the access date, with no unincorporated amendments recorded for this title.

## Original summary

For a primary subclass 500 applicant, the instrument specifies ways to evidence travel, living, course, and accompanying-family costs. The compilation shows annual living costs of AUD$29,710 for the primary applicant, AUD$10,394 for a spouse or de facto partner, AUD$4,449 for a dependent child, and annual school costs of AUD$13,502 for a school-age dependant, subject to stated qualifications and pro-rating. An alternative personal-income route requires official government income documentation showing at least AUD$87,856 without a secondary applicant or AUD$102,500 with one. Specified evidence forms include financial-institution deposits or loans, government loans, scholarships, and financial support.

## Consequential claims supported

- **Claim:** The directly reviewed compilation specifies the listed living, family, school, and income amounts for covered subclass 500 financial-capacity evidence.
  - **Jurisdiction:** Australia
  - **Applicable date:** Amendments compiled and in force from 2024-05-10; current status confirmed 2026-07-15
  - **Accessed:** 2026-07-15
  - **Limitations:** Applicant type, stay length, amounts already paid, family composition, exemptions, and pro-rata rules alter the calculation.
- **Claim:** Specified evidence forms include deposits, financial-institution loans, government loans, scholarships, or financial support.
  - **Jurisdiction:** Australia
  - **Applicable date:** Compilation in force from 2024-05-10
  - **Accessed:** 2026-07-15
  - **Limitations:** The instrument must be read with the Migration Regulations and current application procedures; evidence form does not guarantee sufficiency or visa grant.

## Limitations

The instrument addresses financial-capacity evidence, not every subclass 500 criterion. This record summarizes the primary-applicant provisions and does not reproduce all secondary-applicant, Student Guardian, transitional, or pro-rata details. Although the Register marked this title current, users must recheck for later amendments before relying on the numeric thresholds.
